### Hi there 👋

I'm Stefan Pfenninger-Lee, Associate Professor of Sustainable Energy Systems at [TU Delft's Faculty of Technology, Policy and Management](https://www.tudelft.nl/en/tpm/).
My research is on the transition to a 100% clean and renewable energy system: understanding the variability of renewables, designing systems that can thrive on this variability, and understanding trade-offs with other dimensions of sustainable development.
I also work on scientific software with a particular focus on open, transparent, and reproducible tools.

### Open-source projects

I have created and maintain open-source scientific software for energy systems research, such as:

- [Calliope](https://github.com/calliope-project/calliope): A multi-scale energy systems modelling framework
- [Renewables.ninja](https://github.com/renewables-ninja): Simulate the output of wind and solar power plants anywhere in the world
- [GSEE](https://github.com/renewables-ninja/gsee): Global Solar Energy Estimator, a fast solar energy simulation library (used in Renewables.ninja)
- [Calligraph](https://github.com/calliope-project/calligraph): Interactively explore and visualise Calliope model results
- [modelling-energy-systems](https://github.com/sjpfenninger/modelling-energy-systems): An in-development, open-source textbook on modelling energy systems
